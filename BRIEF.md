# Training &amp; Discipline — Design Brief

## Brand Synopsis

Training &amp; Discipline is a four-trainer boutique personal-training studio in downtown Palm Springs, owned by 35-year industry veteran Brian Guzman. The studio sits at 707 East Tahquitz Canyon Way, in a charcoal-walled space with a literal disco ball over the squat rack, mid-century yellow velvet armchairs, glass garage doors that open onto the San Jacinto mountains, and a bamboo-floored yoga room. Their differentiator: no membership required — sessions are paid one at a time or in 10/20-packs and booked by appointment through Vagaro. Pricing is transparent and starts at $50 for a 30-minute session.

The brand identity is the most distinctive thing about it: a deliberate riff on the **NYC subway / MTA system**. The studio name reads like a transit headline ("Training & Discipline"), and its abbreviation **TDCSPS** is rendered as colored route bullets — T/D in MTA red, C/S in MTA purple, P/S in MTA yellow — alongside the MTA "exit" arrow icon. Studio interiors carry the motif through painted subway-door wall murals and street-name signage props.

Their audience is Palm Springs locals (with a strong, explicit welcome to the LGBTQA+ community per Emma's bio), seasonal-home owners and travelers, and clients with medical/recovery considerations who need programming that works alongside their PT, chiropractor, or surgeon. The tone is **welcoming and inclusive** — the studio name sounds tougher than the brand actually is.

## Design Decisions

### Direction A — Transit Editorial

**The maximalist lean into the subway brand DNA.** Massimo Vignelli energy. Black ground throughout, route-bullet IA, oversized Helvetica-style display, station-board sticky nav, route-line vertical timeline for programs. High-contrast magazine layout density. Trainer specialties tagged with route bullets that color-code their disciplines.

- **Mood:** A 1970s Vignelli MTA poster meets a 2020s editorial broadsheet.
- **Fonts:** **Archivo Black** (display, all caps) + **Inter Tight** (body, 400/500/700) + **JetBrains Mono** (uppercase labels). Google Fonts links: [Archivo Black](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Archivo+Black), [Inter Tight](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter+Tight), [JetBrains Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono).
- **Colors:**
  - Brand black `#0A0A0A` (canvas), pure white `#FFFFFF` (type)
  - MTA red `#EE352E` (T, D bullets)
  - MTA purple `#A626AA` (C, S bullets)
  - MTA yellow `#FCCC0A` (P, S bullets — also used for the marquee + "first consultation complimentary" CTA)
  - Tile grid charcoal `#0D0D0D` (programs section bg)
- **Layout patterns deployed:**
  1. Sticky **MTA station-board nav** — black bar with white horizontal rules above/below, route bullets inline next to each section name, scroll-x on mobile
  2. **Editorial 12-col asymmetric magazine grid** — VOL./ISSUE masthead, eyebrow + display headline + meta stats row
  3. **Route-line vertical timeline** — programs as "stops" on a line, each with a route bullet pin, mono eyebrow ("Stop 01 · Strength"), display headline, supporting body
  4. **MTA station-card contact block** — bordered box with header rule, address typography, route bullets in footer
  5. **Oversized typographic footer** — "Training & Disc[T]p[D]ne." with route bullets baked in mid-word
- **Signature move:** the route bullets are baked **into the headline typography itself** — "Palm Sp[T]ings trains he[C]e." and the footer's "Training & Disc[T]p[D]ne." treat the colored discs as letterforms.

### Direction B — Boutique Desert

**The Palm Springs side of the brand.** Hospitality-grade, soft and editorial. Sand canvas, Fraunces serif with terracotta italics, generous whitespace, alternating image/text editorial spreads for trainers. The transit identity is preserved as a quiet system (route bullets next to specialty labels, brand colors as small accents) but never the headline.

- **Mood:** A small luxury Palm Springs hotel landing page that happens to be about training. Korakia/Parker register, not Equinox.
- **Fonts:** **Fraunces** (display serif, regular + italic, with optical sizing) + **Inter Tight** (body). Google Fonts links: [Fraunces](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Fraunces), [Inter Tight](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter+Tight).
- **Colors:**
  - Sand `#F4ECE0` (canvas), paper `#FAF6EE` (alternating sections)
  - Warm ink `#14110D` (text), soft ink `#2B2722`
  - **Desert terracotta `#B8593E`** (primary accent — italicised words, hover, terracotta consult card)
  - Studio charcoal `#14110D` (deep "trainers" section is full-bleed dark for contrast)
  - Route-bullet trio reserved for the system (specialty labels, footer)
  - MTA yellow `#FCCC0A` reappears in the dark section as the "Pick a trainer / pick a time" booking card
- **Layout patterns deployed:**
  1. **Editorial hero** with vertical photo aside (open glass doors of the studio) and quiet caption "The studio, this morning. — N° 01"
  2. **Marginalia column + body** — about section uses a sticky aside with italicised pull-quote
  3. **Numbered editorial program list** with row hover, mixed serif/italic display, and route-bullet specialty tags
  4. **Alternating image/text trainer spreads** — full-bleed dark canvas, each trainer gets a side, photos go to grayscale-de-saturate by default
  5. **Quiet pricing tables** with serif numerals and bordered rows
  6. **Masonry studio gallery** with column-balance layout
- **Signature move:** **the italic terracotta accent.** Display copy keeps a serif Fraunces base, but a single pivotal word per heading turns italic and terracotta — "Train in *the desert*" / "*unlike a gym*" / "*— amazing*." It carries the warmth of the brand without losing the editorial restraint.

### Direction C — Now Boarding (FRONTEND-FX)

**The kinetic transit-IA direction.** The MTA route metaphor as the literal scroll spine — a vertical service line on the left edge with stops (Hero / About / Programs / Trainers / Pricing / Voices / Studio / Remote / Visit) marked with TDCSPS-colored route bullets that activate as you scroll. Pricing renders as a departure board. Section transitions framed like train-window reveals. Welcoming and editorial — never cold.

- **Mood:** A magazine editorial × MTA route map. A station coming alive as you walk in.
- **Fonts:** **Fraunces** (variable optical serif, 9..144 opsz, with italics — the editorial elevation) + **Archivo Black** (transit-signage moments — route labels, station bars) + **Instrument Sans** (body) + **JetBrains Mono** (departure board, ticker, smallcaps labels). Google Fonts: [Fraunces](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Fraunces), [Archivo Black](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Archivo+Black), [Instrument Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Instrument+Sans), [JetBrains Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono).
- **Colors:**
  - Paper cream `#F5F1E8` (canvas — warm, not cold)
  - Paper deep `#EFE9DA` (alt section)
  - Ink `#0F0F12` (text), Ink-soft `#2A2A30`
  - Rule `#E0D9C6` (hairlines on cream), Line `#2B2B30` (rules on dark)
  - **MTA red `#EE352E`** (T, D bullets — Strength + Discipline lines)
  - **MTA purple `#A626AA`** (C, S bullets — Care + Stillness lines)
  - **MTA yellow `#FCCC0A`** (P, S bullets — Pathway + Studio-to-you lines)
  - Sunset `#E8693C` (CTA hover)
- **Layout patterns deployed:**
  1. **MTA bar announcement ticker** — black bar with thin horizontal rules above/below, monospace caps scrolling: "ALL ABOARD ◆ NOW BOARDING DISTINCTIVE PERSONAL TRAINING ◆ NO MEMBERSHIP REQUIRED ◆ BY APPOINTMENT ◆ 760-904-4111 ◆ 707 E TAHQUITZ CANYON WAY"
  2. **Route legend (left rail, desktop only)** — a vertical line at left:24px with 9 stops, each a 14px circle outline. Active station fills + scales 1.25× with a 5px box-shadow halo. Names hide until hover. A red `progress-fill` bar tracks scroll percentage. Below `lg` the legend hides cleanly.
  3. **Editorial split hero** — left col (7/12) with station-chip "Stop 01 · Now Boarding," giant Fraunces headline ("Palm Springs destination for *distinctive* personal training."), body, three departure-board summary cards (Hours / Format / Membership). Right col (5/12) with the team photo in a `train-window` frame (subtle horizontal vignette overlay, like a moving train window).
  4. **Service-line strip** — black MTA bar after hero showing all 6 routes with their bullets and one-line role: "T · Strength · Beginner to elite", etc.
  5. **Programs as a 6-card route grid** — each program card has its route bullet at large size, monospace eyebrow ("Line · Strength"), display headline, body, dashed-rule footer with cadence note.
  6. **Trainers as a 4-up portrait grid** — each portrait in a `train-window` frame, name + colored route bullet next to it, smallcaps role, condensed credential paragraph.
  7. **Pricing as a "Departure board" panel** — black rounded card with the live header ("LIVE · DEPARTURE BOARD"), a 3-up "single sessions" row (30/45/60 min · $50/$75/$100), then a divided board with 6 pack rows: monospace train code (`10·30M`, `20·30M`, ...), route bullet, description, per-session rate, big Fraunces price. Bottom highlight cell: complimentary new-client consult.
  8. **Editorial pull-quote testimonials** — large red opening quote glyph for T. Kramer's "amazing" pull, smaller purple quote for J. Heersink's longer attestation.
  9. **Studio bento gallery** — 6 unique studio photos in a 12-col asymmetric grid (16/10 wide, 4/5 portraits), every tile in a train-window frame.
  10. **"Bring the Studio to You" three-step card row** — three numbered route-bullet cards (1/2/3), $100/billed every 4 weeks display.
  11. **Visit / Contact slab** — full-bleed black, 96px Fraunces address ("707 East *Tahquitz Canyon* Way · Suite 1"), inline route map with all six TDCSPS bullets, hours/phone/booking grid.
  12. **Big-type ghost footer** — credit row + a continuous slow ticker of "TRAINING & DISCIPLINE · NOW BOARDING · PALM SPRINGS · NO MEMBERSHIP REQUIRED" at 12vw in 6% opacity white as a textural baseline.
- **Signature move:** **the live route line + station legend on the left edge.** It is the page's literal spine. As you scroll, the active stop pulses, the connecting line fills with red, and clicking a stop smooth-scrolls to that section. The transit metaphor isn't decoration — it's the navigation system.
- **Motion:** CSS-only on-load entrance (animation `revealUp` with staggered `--delay-1/2/3` on each section's primary block, keeps the first 1.3s of the page feeling like a station coming alive). Top-bar ticker auto-scrolls (38s linear). Footer ghost-type ticker (60s). All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. No JS-gated reveals — page is always visible if anything fails.

### Direction D — After Hours (FRONTEND-FX)

**The dark-mode boutique-disco hospitality direction.** Palm Springs at 10pm with the lights low. The disco ball is the unforgettable element — radial amber + mauve glows breathe across every section, a soft floor-light sweep drifts under the hero, and tiny mirror sparkles dot the canvas. The transit grid persists as a subtle skeleton (route bullets as content tags) but atmosphere dominates.

- **Mood:** A late-night Palm Springs hotel page that happens to be a boutique gym. Cinematic, intimate, smoldering.
- **Fonts:** **Bodoni Moda** (high-contrast didone display, regular + italic, opsz 6..96 — the late-night boutique register) + **Archivo Black** (transit signage moments only, sparingly) + **Manrope** (body — characterful, warm sans) + **JetBrains Mono** (smallcaps, board, ticker). Google Fonts: [Bodoni Moda](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Bodoni+Moda), [Archivo Black](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Archivo+Black), [Manrope](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Manrope), [JetBrains Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono).
- **Colors:**
  - Ink `#0A0A0E` (canvas — midnight, not pure black), Ink-2 `#11111A`
  - Velvet `#2A1A3D` (deep accent surface, pulled from the lounge purple door)
  - **Amber `#FFB547`** (disco-ball highlight), **Amber-soft `#FFD78A`** (halo edge)
  - **Mauve `#FF6F91`** (disco refraction, secondary glow)
  - Cream `#F5E8D0` (warm body text on dark), Cream-soft `#E8DBC2`
  - Lines: `rgba(245,232,208,.14)` (primary), `rgba(245,232,208,.08)` (secondary)
  - MTA red/purple/yellow used **sparingly** as content tags (program/trainer route bullets only — never for headlines)
- **Layout patterns deployed:**
  1. **LED amber announcement ticker** — pinstripe top bar with amber-tinted monospace caps scrolling: "AFTER HOURS · OPEN BY APPOINTMENT ◆ NO MEMBERSHIP REQUIRED ◆ 760·904·4111".
  2. **Cinematic hero with disco-glow stack** — three radial glows (giant 1100px amber-mauve breathing every 7s, 600px purple every 11s reverse, 500px secondary) layered behind the headline, plus 3 mirror "sparkles" at fixed positions. A 240px floor-sweep with a drifting amber bar runs along the bottom.
  3. **Massive Bodoni hero** — 136px italic display "A boutique studio with a *disco ball* over the squat rack." with a live amber-to-mauve gradient on "*disco ball*" (animation `slideGrad` 14s). Right col: real `studio-disco.jpg` in a `photo-frame-glow` (1px amber ring, deep mauve drop shadow), with a `glass` floating caption "N° 01 · A real disco ball over a real Rogue rack."
  4. **Glass-warm stat strip** — 3 cards (Sessions $50 / Format 1:1 / Members "*none.*") on a backdrop-blur amber-tinted glass surface.
  5. **Two-column About** — Bodoni headline left, body + glass stat cards right (35+ Years · 4 Trainers · "always" 1:1).
  6. **6-program glass card grid** — each card a `glass` panel with a large route bullet (red T/D, purple C/S, yellow P/S), Bodoni program name, body, hover-border to amber.
  7. **4-trainer halo grid** — each photo in a `photo-frame` with a hover-only amber radial halo behind the card (`trainer-halo::before` opacity transition).
  8. **Dark "departure board" pricing** — `board` panel: amber-tinted glass + 1px amber ring + mauve drop shadow. 3-up single sessions in glass-warm cards with monospace train codes, then a 6-row divided board with `board-time` mono codes in amber, route bullets, descriptions, prices. "Complimentary." flagged with the live amber-to-mauve gradient.
  9. **Two-card testimonials** — both `glass-warm` rounded-3xl cards. Big amber QuoteIcon glyph for T. Kramer's pull; mauve QuoteIcon for J. Heersink. Mirror sparkle peeks out of the corner of the bigger card.
  10. **Studio bento (5 unique shots)** — `photo-frame` tiles with subtle saturate(1.05) brightness(1.05) treatment so the desert light reads warm.
  11. **Three-step Remote section** — three `glass-warm` cards numbered with red/purple/yellow route bullets ("1", "2", "3"), $100/4-weeks priced in the gradient italic.
  12. **Cinematic visit/contact slab** — bottom-positioned amber disco glow bleeds into the hero copy, "Tahquitz Canyon" rendered in the amber-mauve gradient italic, glass-warm contact card with the full TDCSPS service map row.
  13. **Glow footer with halos** — two soft amber+mauve halos (`disco-glow-2`) breathing behind a thin credit row, then a big slow ghost-type ticker beneath.
- **Signature move:** **the disco-ball glow that breathes.** Three layered radial gradients with `discoBreathe` keyframe (7s and 11s, opposite phases) animate scale/opacity, so the page feels like the inside of the studio at 10pm with the disco ball turning. Three mirror `disco-sparkle` dots blink at varied delays, and a `floor-sweep` drifts a soft amber bar across the hero floor every 10s. Amber-to-mauve linear-gradient text pulse on the pivot words ties the whole system to the headline.
- **Motion:** All decorative — CSS-only, paused under `prefers-reduced-motion`. Tickers auto-scroll. Disco glows + sparkles + floor-sweep loop continuously. On-load `revealUp` staggered entrance for primary blocks. Trainer photos get a hover halo. CTA buttons translate -2px on hover with shadow lift. Page is never invisible.
